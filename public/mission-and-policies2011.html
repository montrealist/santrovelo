SantroVélo Mission & Policies


Mission:
SantroVélo is a do-it-yourself community bike shop that encourages cycling through sharing bicycle repair facilities, knowledge and enthusiasm. Specifically, we aim to allow underserved groups such as women, youth, people of color and people from low-income communities to access cycling and bicycle repair. SantroVélo also maintains the fleet of bicycles used to deliver meals for Santropol Roulant.

Background:
SantroVélo is part of Santropol Roulant, a Montreal-based non-profit community organization that cooks and delivers meals to people with loss of autonomy. Among its diverse activities, Santropol Roulant integrates its meals-on-wheels program, bike shop, garden and compost programs in a holistic manner. For example, the garden program grows produce for the meals and SantroVélo maintains a fleet of bikes that can be used to deliver the meals.

Membership:
To become a member of SantroVélo, we ask that you fill out the membership form and pay a suggested $15 per year, renewable every year starting April 1. Memberships are non-refundable and non-transferable. Non-members may also use the shop for a suggested $5 per use. No one will be turned away for lack of funds.

User Policies:

  1. In order to use the shop, you must be accompanied by a SantroVélo volunteer or staff member.

  2. This is a do-it-yourself bicycle repair space. While you are encouraged to ask questions and seek help from the volunteers, we encourage you to fix your own bike

  3. This is an inclusive space. Sexist, racist, homophobic or otherwise offensive behavior is not tolerated.

  4. We do sell used bicycles in various states of disrepair for you to fix up or rebuild. As a guideline, a complete bicycle will cost you about $60 including your labor and any new or used parts from the shop. The actual price is at the discretion of the volunteer on duty. We want you to be able to afford a bike. Any new parts cost extra and above the cost of a used bike.

  5. SantroVélo is not a retail shop. As a general rule, any new or used parts purchased from SantroVélo must be installed at the shop.

  6. Put all tools back in their intended places and clean up after yourself before you leave. If you’re not sure where to put something, ask a volunteer or staff member.

  7. If you break or damage a tool, tell a volunteer or staff member.

  8. If you need to leave your unfinished bike at SantroVélo, you must tag it with your name, phone number, email address and the date you left it, and hang it in the storage room downstairs (if there is space available). You may continue to repair and leave the same bike for up to 30 days. After 30 days, it will become property of SantroVélo.
